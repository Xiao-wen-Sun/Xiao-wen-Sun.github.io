I am currently a final-year Ph.D. student in the Knowledge Technology Research (WTM) group in the Department of Informatics, Faculty of Mathematics, Informatics and Natural Sciences at the University of Hamburg, under the supervision of Prof. Dr. [Name].

I recently submitted my doctoral thesis, “Active Clarification in Human–Robot Interaction via Object State Perception and Reasoning.” My research focuses on identifying communication challenges arising from uncertainty, vagueness, and ambiguity in everyday human–robot interactions. By analyzing the underlying sources of these challenges, I developed frameworks to detect, clarify, and resolve them as they arise. Furthermore, this work aims to facilitate the development of human-like, object state–level interactions by endowing robots with advanced visual perception capabilities, thereby supporting more effective human–robot interaction (HRI).

My methods include training individual LSTM networks, applying chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting to foundation models, large language models (LLMs), and vision–language models (VLMs), as well as fine-tuning VLMs for robotic tasks.

I am currently seeking a postdoctoral researcher or postdoctoral research fellow position in computational linguistics, embodied agents, and human–robot interaction (HRI). I am open to opportunities in Germany, Switzerland, France, Italy, Denmark, and Norway.

Previously, I obtained my Bachelor’s degree from Shandong Agricultural University in 2016 and my Master’s degree in Computer Technology from China Agricultural University in 2020.
